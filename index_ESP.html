Resumen1

  • VICTORIA fue un ensayo de fase III, aleatorizado, doble ciego y controlado con placebo, en el que se evaluaron la eficacia y la seguridad de vericiguat en pacientes con fracción de eyección (FE) reducida e insuficiencia cardíaca (IC) crónica con IC descompensada reciente.
  • Durante una mediana de 10.8 meses, el criterio principal de valoración compuesto de muerte por causas cardiovasculares (MCV) u hospitalización por insuficiencia cardíaca (HIC) se produjo en 897/2526 (35.5%) del grupo tratado con vericiguat y en 972/2524 (38.5%) del grupo tratado con placebo (Hazard Ratio de 0.90; intervalo de confianza [IC] del 95%: 0.82 a 0.98; P=0.02).
  • Las tasas de acontecimientos adversos (AA) fueron similares entre vericiguat y placebo.
  • Con respecto a los acontecimientos adversos de interés clínico previamente especificados, se observaron diferencias no significativas en las tasas de hipotensión sintomática (9.1% frente a 7.9%, respectivamente) y síncope (4% frente a 3.5%, respectivamente) con vericiguat y placebo, respectivamente.


Methods1

  • The patient population included subjects >18 years of age with chronic HF (NYHA functional classes II to IV) and reduced EF of < 45%, elevated levels of natriuretic peptides, and a previous HF decompensation (defined as HFH within previous 6 months or use of intravenous diuretics for HF without hospitalization within previous 3 months).
  • Subjects also had elevated natriuretic peptide levels within 30 days prior to randomization.


Patient Characteristics1

  • 5050 patients were enrolled in VICTORIA and followed for a median of 10.8 months.
  • Use of concomitant guidelines-based medical therapy was well balanced: 60% of patients received triple therapy (a beta blocker and a mineralocorticoid antagonist combined with either an ACEI, an ARB, or an ARNI), and 15% received ARNI therapy.
  • The baseline characteristics were well-matched between the two groups.

Primary Efficacy Endpoint1

  • The annual event rate of the primary composite endpoint of CVD or HFH in the vericiguat treatment group was 33.6% vs 37.8% in the placebo group, resulting in a 10% relative hazard reduction (ARR 4.2 per 100 patient-years). The primary endpoint occurred in 897 patients (35.5%) in the vericiguat treatment group compared with 972 patients (38.5%) in the placebo group (HR 0.90; 95% CI 0.82 to 0.98; P=0.02).

Secondary Efficacy Endpoints1

  • Of the individual components of the primary endpoint, CVD occurred in 414 (16.4%) patients in the vericiguat group and 441 (17.5%) in the placebo group (HR 0.93; 95% CI 0.81 to 1.06). HFH occurred in 691 (27.4%) patients receiving vericiguat and 747 (29.6%) receiving placebo (HR 0.90; 95% CI 0.81 to 1.00).

Safety1

  • Rates of AEs (serious and nonserious) were similar between vericiguat and placebo (80.5% vs 81.0%). Rates of serious adverse events were also similar between vericiguat and placebo (32.8 vs 34.8%).
  • For the prespecified adverse events of clinical interest - small differences in the rates of symptomatic hypotension (9.1% vs 7.9%, respectively; P=0.12) and syncope (4% vs 3.5%, respectively; P=0.30) were observed with vericiguat and placebo.
